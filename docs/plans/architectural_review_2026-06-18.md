## Verdict (one paragraph)
The range holds up — **more coherent, not less.** The single defining design choice — *the offline auditor re-runs the gate's own `verify_commit_against_kel_scoped` over raw signed commit bytes, summing agent-signed costs, trusting neither operator nor gateway* — is implemented once in `audit.rs` and reached identically by the hermetic replay path, the live wire, and the standalone `verify-spend` CLI. The shared primitives are genuinely shared: `commit_trailer`, `call_commit_binding`, `SPEND_LOG_GENESIS`, the `CommitVerdict→Verdict` map, `spend_log_path`/`read_spend_log`, and the `CrossRailBudget` are each single-sourced. A new engineer can hold the shape in their head: *sign the call → gate.judge (reserve) → forward → extract actual cost from the rail → settle → sign settlement → append a back-linked record → anyone re-audits offline.* The bloat is real but bounded and mostly **at the edges**: the per-call orchestration is duplicated once (replay vs proxy), one rail constant leaked into the proxy, one doc-comment now lies, and the 5 `.mjs` examples are ~270 lines of copy-paste. None of these fragment the core; they are edge accretion to file as debt, with one worth doing now (the USDC leak). The codebase did NOT bloat past the point of comprehension — you do not need to read 13 commits to understand the gateway; you need to read `audit.rs` and one of {`replay.rs`, `proxy.rs`}.

## Themes (cross-cutting)

### Theme 1 — The brokered-call orchestration exists twice, but the duplication is shallow and self-documenting (the big one)
**What/where:** `replay.rs::drive_call` (lines 223–465) and `proxy.rs::call_tool` (lines 381–591) both run the identical 8-step spine: build cost → `sign_call` → `gate.judge` (reserve) → forward → extract actual → `gate.settle` → `sign_settlement` → `append` + advance back-link. They reuse every *primitive* (`Chain::sign_call`/`sign_settlement`, `PerCallGate::judge`/`settle`, `spend_log::append`, `call_commit_binding`) but not the *sequencing*.
**Why it costs:** ~120 lines of structurally-parallel control flow in two files. A change to the spine (e.g. a new step, or reordering settle-before-persist) must land in both, and a divergence is silent — exactly the failure mode that already bit the live wire (the now-stale `audit.rs` NOTE, Theme 4, records a period where the live path did NOT sign while replay did).
**The divergence is real, though:** replay is transcript-driven (cost from `resolve_call_cost` reading a recorded fixture under `AUTHS_MCP_RAIL_FIXTURES`, verdicts asserted against `call.expect`, the `*_TAMPER`/`*_REBIND` adversarial hooks live *inside* the loop) while proxy is live-MCP-driven (cost from the live `CallToolRequestParam`, actual from the real downstream response, no expectation to assert, no tamper hooks). The two cost-resolution front-halves (`replay::resolve_call_cost` lines 49–94 vs `proxy::call_cost` lines 299–345) genuinely differ in *source* and share almost nothing.
**Verdict: file-as-debt, do NOT factor now.** A shared `brokered_call(ctx, cost: CallCost) -> Record` is *possible* — the back-half (sign → judge → forward-via-closure → settle → sign_settlement → append) is identical — but the seam is contaminated by (a) the tamper hooks that must inject between sign and judge / after sign_settlement, and (b) the `forward` step being a fixture-stub in replay vs a real async downstream call in proxy. Forcing a shared function would push the tamper hooks and the forward-closure into the signature as flags/callbacks, trading 120 honest duplicated lines for a parameterized abstraction with two callers and three injection points — the speculative-generality trap. Revisit ONLY if a third caller appears (a non-replay non-live path) or the tamper hooks move out of `drive_call` into the harness. **The seam, if ever taken:** a `BrokeredCall` struct holding `{idx, tool_call, cost: CallCost, prev_binding}` with a method `fn settle_and_record(&self, chain, gate, budget, forward: impl FnOnce() -> Fut) -> Record` — sign+judge+forward+settle+sign_settlement+append — leaving cost-resolution and tamper-injection caller-side.

### Theme 2 — One rail constant leaked out of the rail abstraction into the agnostic proxy (simplify now)
**What/where:** `rail.rs:135` defines `const USDC_ATOMIC_PER_CENT: u64 = 10_000` (private) and owns all x402/USDC-decimal knowledge (`extract_x402`, the testnet-only guard, the exact-division sub-cent refusal). But `proxy.rs:348–351` re-hardcodes the same `10_000` as a magic literal in a *second*, *different-rounding* helper `atomic_usdc_to_cents_ceiling` (`div_ceil` for the reserve ceiling, vs `rail.rs`'s exact-division-only for the settle). The proxy reads `amount_atomic` off the agent's request (proxy.rs:308–311) and converts it itself.
**Why it costs:** the `atomic_usdc_to_cents` and "USDC has 6 decimals" fact now lives in two crates with two rounding rules. If USDC ever changes decimals, or a second 6-decimal rail is added, the proxy silently mis-meters the *reserve ceiling* while `rail.rs` correctly meters the *settle* — and the divergence is invisible because they round differently *by design*, so a reviewer can't tell a bug from the intended ceiling-vs-exact split. This is a true abstraction leak: the rail-specific decimal count is in `proxy.rs` (rail-agnostic by its own doc, line 204–212).
**Verdict: simplify-now.** Make `rail.rs` export the ceiling conversion: `pub const USDC_ATOMIC_PER_CENT` + `pub fn atomic_usdc_to_cents_ceiling(atomic) -> u64 { atomic.div_ceil(USDC_ATOMIC_PER_CENT) }`, and have `proxy.rs:311` call `auths_mcp_core::rail::atomic_usdc_to_cents_ceiling`. Deletes proxy.rs:348–351, removes the magic literal, and keeps both rounding rules co-located in the one module that owns the rail's decimals. ~5 lines moved, the leak sealed. **This is THE refactor (below).**

### Theme 3 — The new CLI surface is converging, not fragmenting; the env-var hooks are disciplined
**What/where:** the range adds one subcommand (`VerifySpend`, main.rs:66/157–175) and two `wrap` flags (`--rail` main.rs:91–96, and `--agent-delegation` main.rs:120–125). `VerifySpend` is the *user-facing* form of the exact audit `replay.rs` already runs internally (replay.rs:198 `gate.audit_spend_log`) and that the live wire prints a ready-to-run command for (proxy.rs:812–818) — three entrypoints, ONE audit. `--rail` is operator-set (so an agent can't bypass metering by omitting a per-call field, proxy.rs:303–318) and is the live-wire twin of replay's `AUTHS_MCP_RAIL_FIXTURES` fixture mechanism.
**Why it (barely) costs:** the three `*_TAMPER`/`SETTLE_REBIND` env hooks (replay.rs:255, 351, 330) each isolate a *distinct* attack (forge proof / alter signed cost / re-bind a valid settlement onto another call) and run.sh documents *why* REBIND is not subsumed by SETTLE_TAMPER (the cost-alteration breaks the signature before binding is ever checked). That is a designed matrix, not accretion. The only smell is **naming drift across the two harnesses**: the live `.mjs` orchestrators key off `AUTHS_MCP_LIVE_DIR` (proxy.rs:184) + `--rail`, while `run.sh`'s replay path keys off `LAB_DIR` (replay.rs:104) + `AUTHS_MCP_RAIL_FIXTURES` — same two concepts (sandbox root, rail selection), two vocabularies a reader can't grep across.
**Verdict: leave-it.** The surface converges on the one audit; `--agent-delegation` is the documented seam for the deferred live-agent binding (proxy.rs:194–202), not speculative. File the LAB_DIR/AUTHS_MCP_LIVE_DIR naming drift as cosmetic debt.
